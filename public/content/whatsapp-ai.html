WhatsApp AI Assistant

Overview

The WhatsApp AI Assistant is a conversational AI built right into WhatsApp that lets you manage your entire DHS business from your phone using simple text messages. Instead of switching between apps, you can search for clients, create appointments, send invoices, check your schedule, and much more, all from a WhatsApp chat.

With the WhatsApp AI Assistant you can:

  • Search and look up clients, jobs, estimates, invoices, crews, and more
  • Create new records like appointments, clients, jobs, estimates, and invoices
  • Update and manage existing records — change statuses, reschedule, edit fields
  • Track time — clock in, clock out, and review timesheets
  • Communicate — send messages and notifications to your team or clients
  • Get reports — daily briefings, schedule summaries, and business reports
  • Use utilities — check the weather, get directions, run quick calculations

The assistant supports over 103 tools and works in both English and Spanish. You can type your requests in natural, everyday language — no special commands to memorize.

Getting Started

Linking Your WhatsApp Number

Before you can use the WhatsApp AI Assistant, you need to link your WhatsApp phone number to your DHS account. Here is how:

  1. Open the DHS app on your phone or desktop.
  2. Go to Settings and select WhatsApp Integration.
  3. Enter the phone number associated with your WhatsApp account.
  4. Tap Link Number. You will receive a verification code via WhatsApp.
  5. Enter the verification code in the DHS app to confirm the link.
  6. Once verified, you can start messaging the DHS AI Assistant directly on WhatsApp.

Authentication and Security

Your account is protected.

Only the phone number you link to your DHS account can talk to the assistant. If anyone else messages it from a different number, they won't be able to see any of your information.

If you haven't used the assistant in a while, we may ask you to verify it's really you. When that happens, you'll get an email with a link — tap it, and you can pick up right where you left off.

Opening Things in the App from Chat

The assistant understands four kinds of commands:

  • Open — returns a one-tap link to the app.
  • Show / View — displays results inline in the chat. No link. ("view" is an alias for "show".)
  • Create — creates a record directly in the chat; the assistant prompts for any missing details. No link.
  • Open Create — returns a one-tap link to the blank create form, for the times you would rather fill out the page yourself.

When the assistant sends you a link (Open or Open Create), that link does three things at once:

  1. Logs you in automatically — no password screen, no waiting.
  2. Drops you exactly where you need to be — the right job, list, or form is already loaded.
  3. Stays clean in your messages — it is a short link that fits in a single text bubble.

1. Open — Jump to a specific record

Use this when you want to open a specific job, estimate, invoice, client, or other record you have been discussing.

You Say What Happens
"open it" Opens whatever you were just talking about
"open this" Same thing — opens the most recent item from your chat
"open 3" After the assistant lists 5 jobs, opens the third one
"open the Henderson estimate" Looks up the Henderson estimate by name and opens it
"chat with Maria" Opens your inbox conversation with that client
"open chat with Maria" Same — both phrasings open the inbox with that client

Why both chat phrasings work: Both "chat with Maria" and "open chat with Maria" take you to the same place — Maria's conversation in your inbox, where you can send her a message.

Why use it? You are mid-conversation about a specific record and you want to see it, edit it by hand, attach a photo, or just confirm what you are looking at. One tap and you are there.

2. Open — Browse an entire section

Use this when you want to browse a whole section of the app — the full list of jobs, estimates, clients, etc. — instead of any one specific record.

Use this when you would rather fill out the form by hand in the app instead of dictating the record to the AI. Say "open create" followed by the entity type. This is the only phrasing that returns a form link — bare "new estimate" or "create a job" triggers the AI to capture details in chat (see "Create records inline vs. Open Create", below).

Why use it? Some records are easier to create with the full form in front of you — especially when there are dropdowns, file attachments, multiple line items, or custom fields. Open Create skips the navigation and the "+ Add" click for you.

Create records inline vs. Open Create

The assistant supports both creation workflows — pick the one that fits the task:

  • Create inline — say something like "create a job for Maria Lopez". The assistant asks you for anything else it needs (date, line items, crew) right in the chat, then saves it. Best when you already know the basics and want it done in one go without leaving the conversation.
  • Open Create — say "open create job". The assistant sends you a one-tap link to the blank New Job form in the app. Best when the record is complex — attachments, photos, multiple line items, custom fields — or when you want to look things up as you fill it out.

Important Things to Know

  • Links are one-use only. The first tap logs you in and opens the app. The second tap will not work — you will see a friendly "Link Expired" page asking you to request a new one. This is intentional and is how we keep your account safe. If you tap twice by accident, just ask the assistant to open the same thing again — you will get a brand-new link instantly.
  • Links expire after 24 hours. Even if you never tap a link, it stops working after a day. If you come back to an old conversation and tap an old link, just ask the assistant to send you a fresh one.
  • Links are tied to YOUR account. If you forward a link to a coworker, it will not log them in — it will show "Link Expired" because the link only works for the person who originally requested it. Each team member needs to request their own links.
  • Links stay clean. After you tap a "create" link and the form opens, refreshing or bookmarking the page won't re-open the form again. One-tap, one-action.
  • Works on every channel. The same link works whether you tap it from WhatsApp, SMS, the in-app chat panel, or a web browser preview. If you are already logged in, the link still works — it just skips the login step.

Tips and Tricks

  • 'Open it' is smarter than you think. The assistant remembers what you were just talking about. If you said "find jobs at the Henderson place" and it lists 3 jobs, you can say "open it" (most recent), "open 2" (second in the list), or "open the roof one" (matches by keyword).
  • Numbered lists are your friend. When the assistant gives you a numbered list of results, you can just type the number to act on it — for example, "open 2" after a list of estimates.
  • Use list mode when you want to browse. If you are not sure what you are looking for, "open invoices" is faster than typing search keywords.
  • Use Open Create for complex records. "open create estimate" opens the full form page — use it when you need attachments, multiple line items, or custom fields.
  • Mix it with other AI features. A natural workflow: "find clients in Tucson" → assistant lists 12 → "open 4" → tap link, view notes in the app, tap back to WhatsApp → "create a job for them at 555 Oak Ave".

Common Questions

Question Answer
Can I open something on my computer if I requested the link from my phone? Yes — the link is a normal URL. You can copy it from your phone and paste it on your laptop. But remember: it is still one-use, so if you tap it on your phone first, it will not work on your laptop.
What if I lose my place and want to come back to the app later? Just ask the assistant again. "Open the Henderson estimate" or "open it" (if you were just discussing it) will give you a fresh link.
I tapped a link and it says "Link Expired" — what do I do? Ask the assistant to send you a new one. Just say what you wanted to open. Links expire after 24 hours and after one tap, so this is normal.
Can I open something I do not have permission to see? No. The link is tied to your account and your permissions. If you ask to open something you do not have access to, the assistant will tell you it cannot be found.
Why doesn't the AI just write a normal URL I can copy? The assistant builds every link fresh for your session, so you can be sure it is valid, active, and pointing to the right record. A regular URL you would find on a webpage cannot guarantee any of that.
Can I open someone else's data using their link? No — even if you had their link, it would be tied to their account, not yours. Each user needs their own assistant conversation.
Does this work with the WhatsApp Business account or only the personal one? It works with whichever WhatsApp number is connected to your DHS account. Ask your admin if you are not sure which number to message.

Sending Files and Images

The WhatsApp AI Assistant can handle files and images, making it easy to share documents and get AI-powered visual analysis.

Uploading Attachments

You can send files to the assistant just like you would send them to any WhatsApp contact:

  • Tap the attachment icon (paperclip) in WhatsApp.
  • Select the file you want to send (PDFs, documents, spreadsheets, etc.).
  • The assistant will process the file and let you know what it received.

Image Analysis

Send photos directly to the assistant for AI-powered analysis. This is useful for:

  • Job site photos — snap a picture of a property and ask the assistant to note conditions or create a job description.
  • Document scanning — photograph a handwritten note or printed document and have the assistant extract the information.
  • Issue documentation — send a photo of a problem area and the assistant can help you log it as a note on the relevant job or client record.

Downloading Files

When the assistant sends you a file — an estimate PDF, invoice, or report — it arrives as a link you can tap to view or download on your phone. The link is private to you and expires after a short time, so you don't need to worry about anyone else getting to it.

Security

Your data stays private and protected.

Your phone number is the key

When you first connect WhatsApp to DHS, we verify your phone number so we know it's really you. From then on, only that number can reach the assistant on your account.

Keeping your session safe

If you haven't used the assistant for a while, we'll ask you to verify your identity again — a small precaution in case your phone ends up in the wrong hands. When that happens: the assistant lets you know, we send an email to the address on your DHS account, you tap the link, and you're back in the conversation right where you left off.

Files and downloads

Any file the assistant sends you — a PDF, invoice, or report — arrives as a private link just for you. The link expires after a short time and only works once, so it can't be forwarded or reused.

Your data

  • The assistant only sees records in your own DHS account — never anyone else's.
  • Your messages are private and aren't kept longer than needed to answer you.
  • Everything between WhatsApp and DHS travels encrypted.
