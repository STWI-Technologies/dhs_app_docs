Jobs Management

Overview

Jobs are the core work units of the DHS platform. A job represents a specific piece of work to be performed for a client at a property, assigned to a crew, and scheduled for a particular date and time. Jobs bring together clients, properties, crews, services, products, checklists, and invoices into a single, trackable record.

Jobs follow a status workflow that tracks their progress from initial creation through completion or cancellation. Each job can include services (labor items), products (material items), a checklist of tasks to complete, and related invoices.

From this section you can:

  • Create new jobs with a client, property, crew, and scheduled date/time.
  • Track status through Draft, Scheduled, En Route, Started, Paused, Completed, and Canceled.
  • Manage services and products — add, edit, and remove labor and material line items.
  • Assign checklists — attach a checklist that must be completed before the job can be marked done.
  • Complete jobs with a checklist review process.
  • Reschedule jobs with crew availability checking.
  • Generate invoices from completed jobs.
  • Download PDFs of job details.
  • Manage recurring jobs with repeating schedules.
  • Search and filter by keyword, date range, and status.

Important: A job can only be deleted if it has no linked invoices and no linked timesheet entries. If a job has invoices or timesheet entries attached, the delete button will be disabled and a tooltip will explain why.

Jobs List View

The Jobs List View is the main view for managing all your jobs. To access it, navigate to Menu > Jobs.

At the top of the page you will see:

  • A Search bar — type a keyword to filter jobs by name or details.
  • A Date Range filter — select a predefined range (All Time, Last 7 Days, Last 30 Days, etc.) or a custom date range.
  • A Status filter button — opens a popover with checkboxes for each status. Shows a badge with the count of selected filters. Includes a "Reset filter" button to clear selections.
  • A New Job button — click to open the create job wizard.

Creating a New Job

To create a new job, click the New Job button. A multi-step wizard opens with three steps:

Required

Yes

A descriptive name for the job (for example, "AC Unit Installation" or "Quarterly Maintenance").

The property where the work will be performed. The dropdown is filtered based on the selected client.

Click Next to proceed to Step 2.

Step 2: Start Date

A calendar picker appears where you select the start date for the job. The calendar shows up to one year in advance.

  • Dates where the crew is available are shown normally.
  • Dates where the crew is unavailable (due to availability settings or exceptions) are grayed out.
  • After selecting a date, choose a Start Time from a dropdown of available time slots based on the crew's schedule.

Click Next to proceed to Step 3.

Step 3: End Date

Similar to Step 2, select the end date and time. The end date/time must be after the start date/time.

If the end date/time is before the start, you will see the error: "End date and time must be after start date and time."

Tip: If you need to schedule a job outside the crew's normal availability, you can use the Override Availability option. The system will show a banner indicating whether the selected time is available, tentative, or unavailable for the crew.

Click Create to finish. On success: "Job successfully created."

Job Detail Page

The detail page shows everything about a single job. Click any job in the list to open it. The page has a header, a set of tabs for different sections, and a sidebar with client information and communication tools.

Page Header

  • A Back button — returns to the Jobs List View (or to the appointment/estimate you came from, if applicable).
  • The job title with a recurring badge if the job is part of a recurring series.
  • A Status dropdown — click to change the job's status. When you select "Completed," the Complete Job modal opens instead of changing the status directly.
  • A Timer dropdown — Start, Stop, or Resume a timer to track active work time. This is hidden for Completed and Canceled jobs.
  • A More Actions menu (three dots icon) — opens a menu with additional actions (see More Actions below).

Tabs

Job Status Workflow

Jobs follow a status workflow that tracks their progress. You can change a job's status using the status dropdown in the detail page header or through the More Actions menu.

Status Flow

The typical flow is: Draft → Scheduled → En Route → Started → Completed

However, you can transition from any status to any other status. For example, a Started job can be Paused and then resumed (Started again), or a Scheduled job can be Canceled directly.

Important: When you select Completed from the status dropdown, the system does not change the status immediately. Instead, it opens the Complete Job Modal where you must review and complete the checklist (if one is assigned) before the job can be finalized. See the "Completing a Job" section below for details.

While the status is being updated, a message appears: "Updating status to [New Status]." On success: "Job successfully updated."

Completing a Job

When you change a job's status to Completed (either from the status dropdown or the More Actions menu), the Complete Job Modal opens.

Jobs Without a Checklist

If the job has no checklist assigned, the modal simply asks: "Are you sure you want to complete this job?" Click Complete Job to confirm.

Jobs With a Checklist

If the job has a checklist, the modal displays all checklist items and requires you to review them before completing:

  1. The modal shows: "Before completing review the checklists."
  2. Each checklist item appears with a checkbox. You must check every item to proceed.
  3. If a checklist item has Description Required enabled, a text area appears when you check that item. You must fill in the description before the job can be completed.
  4. If any items are not checked or required descriptions are empty, you will see the error: "This field is required."
  5. The Complete Job button is only enabled when all items are checked and all required descriptions are filled in.

Click Complete Job to finalize, or Cancel to go back without completing.

Tip: If you need to add notes about specific checklist items, the Description Required feature ensures that important details (like serial numbers, readings, or pass/fail notes) are captured before the job is closed.

Editing a Job

To edit a job's core details, click the Edit button on the job detail page or click the edit icon in the list view. A panel opens with the same fields as Step 1 of the create wizard (Client, Title, Property, Checklist, Crew, Priority).

Note: In edit mode, you cannot change the scheduled dates directly. To change dates, use the Reschedule action from the More Actions menu, which opens a dedicated rescheduling wizard with availability checking.

Deleting a Job

To delete a job, click the Delete button on the row in the list view or in the More Actions menu on the detail page.

A confirmation dialog appears with the title Delete and the message: "Are you sure you want to delete this job?"

Click Delete (red button) to confirm, or Cancel to go back.

On success: "Job successfully deleted." On failure: "Failed to delete job."

Warning: Deletion is permanent. You cannot delete a job that has linked invoices or timesheet entries. The system checks for these and disables the delete button if they exist. The tooltip explains the exact counts — for example: "There are 2 invoices linked to this job." or "There is 1 timesheet entry linked to this job." You must remove or delete those linked records first before the job can be deleted.

Recurring Jobs

Jobs can be set up as recurring, meaning they automatically repeat on a schedule (for example, weekly maintenance visits or monthly inspections). Recurring jobs have special features:

  • A recurring badge icon appears next to the job name in the list view, indicating whether the job is a template or an instance of a recurring series.
  • The Recurring Schedule tab on the detail page shows the repeating pattern (using RRULE scheduling rules).
  • Individual instances of a recurring job can be detached from the series to become standalone jobs. This is useful when a specific occurrence needs different handling.
  • Instances can be modified individually without affecting the rest of the series.
  • The entire series can be canceled, or individual instances can be skipped by adding exceptions.

Crew Availability and Scheduling

When creating or rescheduling a job, the system checks the assigned crew's availability:

  • The calendar highlights dates where the crew has available time slots.
  • Dates where the crew is unavailable (due to work day settings, exceptions, or seasonal blocks) are grayed out.
  • A message shows how many dates in the next 90 days have available slots.
  • The system detects schedule conflicts — if the crew already has overlapping jobs scheduled, it will warn you.
  • An Override Availability option lets you schedule the job outside normal hours when needed.
  • A banner indicates whether the selected time is Available, Tentative, or Unavailable.

Cross-Platform Usage

Appointments

Jobs can be created from appointments. When viewing an appointment, you can generate a job that inherits the client, property, and crew information from the appointment.

Estimates

Jobs can also be created from estimates using the Create Job from Estimate feature. This copies the line items, client, and property information from the estimate into the new job.

Invoices

Invoices can be generated from jobs. The Invoices tab on the job detail page shows all related invoices. Services and products from the job carry over to the invoice.

Timesheets

Time entries in timesheets can be linked to specific jobs. When a time entry references a job, the job name appears in the timesheet entry table. Jobs with linked timesheet entries cannot be deleted.

Checklists

When a checklist is assigned to a job, the job gets its own independent copy of the checklist items. Completing or modifying checklist items on the job does not affect the original checklist template. The Checklist tab appears only when a checklist is assigned.

Scheduler and Map

Jobs appear on the Scheduler (calendar view) and Map view based on their scheduled dates and property location. You can navigate to either view directly from the More Actions menu.
