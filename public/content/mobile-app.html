Mobile app

Overview

The Direct Home Service mobile app is the field companion to the web platform. It is built for service providers — technicians, crew leads, and owners — who need to run their day from a phone or tablet: check the schedule, work jobs, capture estimates and invoices, track time, manage the client list and catalog, and stay in touch with clients and the office.

With the mobile app you can:

  • See your day at a glance on the Home dashboard, with your key numbers and upcoming work.
  • Work jobs end to end — travel with directions, run a work timer, add services and products, complete a checklist, and bill the client.
  • Schedule and manage appointments, then convert them into estimates, jobs, or invoices.
  • Create and send estimates and invoices, take payments, and track what is owed.
  • Manage clients, properties, and your catalog of services and products.
  • Track time with automatic and manual timesheet entries.
  • Message clients and your team, and ask the built-in AI Assistant in English or Spanish.

Note: The app adapts to your device — a compact bottom navigation bar on phones (built around a central Work Hub) and a fuller navigation rail on tablets. On the Solo plan, crew-related fields are hidden throughout.

Getting Started

Welcome to the Direct Home Service mobile app — your field companion for running jobs, scheduling, estimates, invoices, and time tracking from your phone or tablet. This article walks you through signing in and finding your way around.

Sign in

  1. Open the app.
  2. Enter the email and password for your DHS account (the same login you use on the web).
  3. Tap Sign In.

Your session is remembered, so you won't need to sign in every time. If your details are wrong or your account is disabled, you'll see an error — contact your administrator if you can't get in.

New here? First-time users see a short intro and, after signing in, a quick welcome tour.

The bottom navigation bar

On a phone, five slots are always visible:

  • Home — your dashboard and daily summary (a badge appears when you have unread alerts).
  • Scheduler — the calendar of your appointments and jobs.
  • Work Hub — the center button; opens Jobs, Appointments, and Estimates.
  • AI — the AI Assistant.
  • More — Clients, Invoices, Timesheets, Services, Products, Settings, Help and Support and sign-out.

The active section is highlighted. Tapping a slot switches to it and remembers where you left off.

The Work Hub

The raised center button is the Work Hub — your day-to-day work lives here. Tap it and choose:

  • Jobs — the work you're doing.
  • Appointments — scheduled visits.
  • Estimates — quotes for clients.

The More menu

Tap More to reach:

  • Clients
  • Invoices
  • Timesheets
  • Services and Products
  • Settings
  • Help & Support (opens the online help center)

The Log Out button is located at the bottom of the screen.

Your Account & Profile

This article covers signing in, updating your profile, changing your password and language, getting help, and signing out.

Create an account

New service providers can sign up from the Sign Up link on the Sign In screen. It's a two-step form:

  1. Personal Information — choose your Plan (Standard or Solo), then enter your name, email, phone, and a password. Passwords must be at least 10 characters with an uppercase letter, a lowercase letter, a number, and a special character.
  2. Company Info — enter your company name, address, zip, time zone, and (optionally) website and up to three service categories.

Tap Sign Up to finish.

Your profile

To view and edit your profile, tap your name/avatar at the top of the Home dashboard to open My Profile.

Here you can:

  • Update your profile picture (Upload Image — 2 MB max; or Remove).
  • Edit your First Name, Last Name, Email, and Phone.
  • View your Role and Time Zone.

Tap Apply Changes to save. You'll see "Your profile has been successfully updated."

Change your password

On the My Profile screen, scroll to Change Password:

  1. Enter your Current Password.
  2. Enter and confirm a New Password (same strength rules as sign-up).
  3. Tap Change Password.

Change your language

On My Profile, under Language, choose English or Español. The app switches immediately.

Tip: The AI Assistant also understands both languages — just type in whichever you prefer.

Help & Support

Tap More > Help & Support to open the online help center in your browser.

Sign out

Tap More, scroll to the bottom, and tap Log Out, then confirm. You'll return to the Sign In screen.

Home Dashboard

The Home dashboard is your starting point each day — a quick summary of your numbers, your jobs, and your open estimates and invoices. This article explains what's on it.

Home is the first screen after signing in and the leftmost slot on the navigation bar. Pull down to refresh it at any time. Tap your name/avatar at the top to open My Profile, and tap the bell icon to see notifications.

Overview cards

Four cards summarize your business at a glance:

  • Total Jobs
  • Total Real Profit
  • Pending Invoices (with a View shortcut)
  • Total Potential Profit

Each card shows a trend chip — green (up) or red (down) versus the previous period, or grey when there's no change.

Jobs

The Jobs section lists your work with Upcoming and Today tabs. Each card shows the job name, status, crew, and scheduled date/time — tap it to open the job. Use View all to jump to the full Jobs list, or Add Job to create one.

Open estimates and invoices

  • Open Estimates — your active quotes, each with its status, crew, and total. Tap View all.
  • Open Invoices — unpaid bills, each with its number, job, status, and total. Tap View all.

Tap any card to open that record.

The work timer

If you have a timer running on a job or appointment, a timer bar appears on the dashboard with a View Job shortcut and a stop control, so you can manage your active time from anywhere.

Notifications

A red dot on the bell icon means you have unread alerts. Tap it to open the notifications list, where you can read them and tap any item to jump straight to the related job, estimate, invoice, or appointment.

Scheduler (Calendar)

The Scheduler is your calendar of appointments and jobs. This article covers switching views, moving through dates, filtering by crew (Standard and Team Plans only), and creating work from the calendar.

Open the Scheduler tab (bottom navigation) to see your appointments and jobs laid out by time, so you can plan your route and your day. Pull down to refresh.

Switch calendar views

Use the tabs at the top to change the view:

  • Day — one day's timeline.
  • Week — a week strip with the day's events.
  • Month — a full month grid; days with events show colored dots and a "+N" when there are several.
  • List — a simple list of the selected day's events.

Move through dates

  • Tap Today to jump back to the current day.
  • Use the back / forward arrows to step by day, week, or month (depending on your view).
  • Tap the date label to open a date picker and jump to any day.

Filter by crew

Use the crew dropdown to focus on one crew's schedule, or choose All Crews to see everything. (Standard and Team Plans only)

Open a job or appointment

Tap any event on the calendar to open it — appointments open the appointment detail, jobs open the job detail. In List view, each row shows the type (Appointment or Job), start/end times, and crew.

Create from the calendar

Tap an empty time slot to open the Create New Event sheet, then choose:

  • New Job — creates a scheduled job at that time.
  • New Appointment — creates a scheduled appointment at that time.

The create form opens pre-filled with the date and time you tapped.

Jobs

Jobs are the work you schedule, track, and bill from your phone. This article covers creating a job, running the work timer, adding services and products, completing the job, and billing your client.

A job is scheduled work assigned to a crew for a client's property. From a job you can travel to the site with directions, log your hours, record the services and products used, complete a checklist, and turn it all into an invoice — without leaving the app.

Note: On the Solo plan, the Crew field is hidden throughout Jobs (a single crew is used automatically).

Open the Jobs list

Tap the Work Hub (the center button of the bottom navigation bar) and choose Jobs.

From here you can:

  • Search by keyword, or tap the filter icon to show only certain statuses.
  • Tap any job card to open it.
  • Tap + (top right) to create a new job.

Each card shows the job name, client, scheduled date/time, crew, and a colored status pill.

Create a job

  1. On the Jobs list, tap +.
  2. Step 1 of 2 – Job Details: choose the Client, Property, and Crew. Optionally pick a Checklist and turn on Priority Job. Tap Next Step.
  3. Step 2 of 2 – Start & End Dates: pick the start date and time, then the end date and time. Turn on Override Business Hours to schedule outside normal hours, or Is Locked to prevent schedule changes. Tap Add Job.

Tip: If the client doesn't exist in your account yet, you can add them directly from the Clients dropdown by selecting Quick Add → New Client.

Track your time (En Route, Work, Stop)

Open a job and use the timer bar. The timer logs your hours and moves the job's status automatically:

  • En Route – opens directions and your ETA, then starts travel time and sets the job to En Route.
  • Start Work Time – begins job time and sets the job to Started.
  • Start Office Time / Job Prep Time – log those hours separately.
  • Stop – ends the running timer.

Note: The timer is hidden once a job is Completed or Canceled.

Add services and products

In the job's Services or Products section, tap + to pick from your existing items — or use Quick Add to create a new one.

  • Services have editable Time (hours/minutes) and Rate.
  • Products have editable Quantity and Price.

Each item shows a running Total. After editing a value, tap Apply Changes to save.

Update the status

Open the job and tap the Status pill to choose a new status (Draft, Scheduled, Pending, Confirmed, En Route, Arrived, Started, Paused, Completed, or Canceled).

Note: Choosing Paused stops the timer. Choosing Completed or Canceled asks you to confirm first.

Complete a job

Set the status to Completed.

  • If the job has a checklist, the Complete Job screen opens first — fill in any required items, then tap Complete Job.
  • If there's no checklist, just confirm.

Completing a job stops any running timer.

Bill your client and create an invoice

  1. In the job's Bill section, review the Subtotal, add Tax, and enter any Discount or Required Deposit. Tap Apply Changes.
  2. Tap More > Create Invoice to generate an invoice from the job and open it.

Note: Create Invoice is only available once the job has at least one service or product.

Reschedule or delete a job

  • Reschedule: open the job, tap More > Reschedule, and pick new start/end dates.
  • Delete: use the ⋮ menu on the job card (or More on the detail screen) and confirm. Deleting a job is permanent.

Appointments

Appointments are scheduled visits with a client — a quote visit, an inspection, or any on-site meeting. This article covers creating an appointment, scheduling it, running the visit timer, and turning it into an estimate, job, or invoice.

An appointment ties a client and property to a date and time, optionally with a crew and the services you expect to perform. From an appointment you can log time on site, then convert the visit into an estimate, job, or invoice once it's complete.

Note: On the Solo plan, the Crew field is hidden throughout.

Open the Appointments list

Tap the Work Hub (center button) and choose Appointments.

  • Use the two tabs — Active and Archived.
  • Search by keyword, or tap the filter icon to filter by status.
  • Tap + (top right) to create one.
  • Tap any card to open it.

Each card shows the appointment name, client, Scheduled for date/time (with a "(Today)" / "(Tomorrow)" hint), crew, and a colored status pill.

Create an appointment

  1. Tap + on the Appointments list.
  2. Step 1 – Enter Details: enter the Client, Property, and Crew (Standard and Team Plans only). Tap Next.
  3. Step 2 – Schedule & Review: set the date, time, and duration (see below), optionally add services, review the summary, and tap Submit.

Tip: Not ready to schedule? Tap Save as Draft on step 2 to save the appointment as a Draft and finish it later.

Schedule (date, time, duration)

On the schedule step:

  1. Tap Select Date and pick a day. Unavailable days are greyed out; if you need one anyway, turn on Override to ignore business hours.
  2. Tap Select Time and choose from the Available times. If the day has none, you'll see "No available time slots."
  3. Choose a Duration — 30 minutes, 1 hour, 1.5 hours, 2 hours, or Custom (enter the minutes).

You can also turn on High Priority or Callback (a callback links the visit to an existing job).

Run the visit timer

Open an appointment (unless it's Completed or Canceled) and use the timer:

  • En Route starts travel time and sets the status to En Route.
  • Start Work starts job time and sets the status to Started.
  • Stop ends the running timer.

Logged time appears in the Visits section of the appointment.

Update the status

Tap the Status pill to change it. Appointments move through: Draft, Scheduled, Pending, Confirmed, En Route, Arrived, Started, Completed, Canceled, and No Show.

Note: Choosing Completed or Canceled asks you to confirm first.

Convert to an estimate, job, or invoice

Open the appointment and go to the Related Records tab. Once the appointment is Completed, you can:

  • Convert to Estimate — creates an estimate from the visit.
  • Convert to Job — schedules a job from the visit.
  • Add Invoice — bills the visit (requires that the appointment has services).

Note: These buttons are disabled until the appointment status is Completed.

Send the appointment to your client

From the appointment details section, open the More menu and select Send to Client. Choose Email, SMS, or both, edit the subject and message, and click Send to Client. Use Preview to see the message first.

Note: Sending is available only once the appointment is scheduled and has a crew, and its status isn't Draft or Completed.

Reschedule, archive, or delete

  • Reschedule: open the appointment, tap More > Reschedule, and pick a new date/time.
  • Set as Priority: toggle it from the More menu.
  • Archive: from the card's ⋮ menu or More, tap Archive and confirm. Archived appointments move to the Archived tab.
  • Restore / Delete: on the Archived tab, use the card's ⋮ menu to Restore or permanently Delete.

Estimates

An estimate is the quote you send a client before doing the work. This article covers creating an estimate, adding services and products, sending it for approval, and converting an approved estimate into a job.

An estimate lists the services and products you propose, with pricing, and can be sent to the client to review, comment on, or approve with a signature. Estimates move through four statuses: Draft, Ready for Review, Changes Requested, and Approved.

Note: Once an estimate is Approved or Ready for Review, its line items and totals are locked from editing. On the Solo plan, the Crew field is hidden.

Open the Estimates list

Tap the Work Hub (center button) and choose Estimates.

  • Search by keyword, or tap the filter icon to filter by status.
  • Tap + (top right) to create one.
  • Tap any card to open it.

Each card shows the estimate name, client, date created, crew, status, and total.

Create an estimate

  1. Tap + on the Estimates list.
  2. Step 1 – Estimate Details: enter the Client, Property, and Crew (Standard and Team Plans only). Tap Next Step.
  3. Step 2 – Quick Add: add the services and products for the quote (see below). Tap Add Estimate.

Tip: If a client or property doesn't exist yet, use the New Client / Property button inside the picker to add it without leaving the form.

Add services and products

In the Services and Products sections, tap + to pick an existing item — or use Quick Add to create a new one on the spot.

  • Services have editable Time (hours/minutes) and Rate.
  • Products have editable Quantity and Price.

Each line shows a running Total. After changing a value, tap Apply Changes.

Adjust the bill (tax, discount, deposit)

Open the estimate and scroll to the Bill section:

  • Tax — tap to pick a tax rate (or remove it).
  • Discount — enter an amount.
  • Required Deposit — enter an amount you want up front.

The Total updates automatically. Tap Apply Changes to save. You can also add Comments to Client that appear on the estimate.

Send the estimate to your client

  1. On the estimate, tap Submit Estimate.
  2. Choose Email, SMS, or both, and edit the subject and message.
  3. Tap Preview to review, then Send estimate.

When you send the estimate, its status changes to Ready for Review. The client receives a link to view and approve it. When they approve and sign, the estimate shows an Approved by block with their signature and the date.

Approve and convert to a job

Once an estimate is Approved, open it and tap Convert to Job:

  1. Pick the Start Date and time.
  2. Pick the End Date and time.
  3. Tap Convert to Job.

A job is created from the estimate's services and products. To edit an approved estimate first, tap Edit Estimate (this moves it back to Changes Requested).

Duplicate or delete

From an estimate card's ⋮ menu (or the estimate's overview menu):

  • Duplicate — creates a copy you can adjust for another client or property.
  • Delete — removes the estimate (confirmation required).

Note: You cannot delete or duplicate estimates that are currently marked as Approved or Ready for Review.

Invoices

An invoice is the bill you send a client for completed work. This article covers creating an invoice, sending it, recording payments, and tracking what's still owed.

An invoice lists the services and products delivered, with pricing and totals, and tracks how much the client has paid. Invoices move through four statuses: Draft, Sent, Viewed, and Paid. Once an invoice is Paid, it becomes a read-only receipt.

Open the Invoices list

Tap More (bottom navigation) and choose Invoices.

  • Search by keyword, or tap the filter icon to filter by status.
  • Tap + (top right) to create one.
  • Invoices are grouped by job — tap Job Details on a group header to open its job.
  • Tap any invoice to open it.

Each invoice shows its number, status, and total.

Create an invoice

  1. Tap + on the Invoices list.
  2. Step 1 – Details: optionally add invoice details, then choose the Client, Property, and Job. Set the Issue Date and Due Date. Tap Next Step.
  3. Step 2 – Quick Add: add or confirm the services and products to bill. Tap Add Invoice.

Tip: Selecting a Job automatically pulls that job's services and products into the invoice.

Adjust the bill (tax, discount, deposit)

Open the invoice's Overview tab and scroll to the Bill section:

  • Tax — tap to pick a tax rate (or remove it).
  • Discount and Required Deposit — enter amounts.

The Total updates automatically. Tap Apply Changes to save.

Send the invoice to your client

  1. On the invoice, tap Send Invoice.
  2. Choose Email, SMS, or both, and edit the subject and message.
  3. Tap Preview to review, then Send invoice.

The client receives a link to view the invoice, which moves the status to Sent (and Viewed once they open it).

Record a payment

  1. Open the invoice and go to the Payments tab.
  2. Tap + to add a payment.
  3. Enter the Date, Type (Credit Card, Bank Transfer, Check, Cash, Payment Processor, or Other), Status, and Amount. Add a reference or note if you like.
  4. Tap Add Payment.

Tip: If there's a balance owed, the amount field pre-fills with the remaining balance and the status defaults to Completed.

Track payments and the balance due

The Payments tab shows a summary — Total, Required Deposit, and Remaining — plus a Payments History list. Use each payment's ⋮ menu to Edit or Delete it.

Mark as paid or delete

  • Mark as paid: tap the Status pill and choose Paid. The invoice becomes a read-only receipt.
  • Delete: from the invoice's More menu (or the list card's ⋮), tap Delete and confirm.

Clients

Clients are the customers you do business with. This article covers finding clients, adding them, managing their properties, and viewing their related work.

A client can be a homeowner (Residential) or a company (Business). Each client can have multiple properties, and all their appointments, estimates, jobs, and invoices are linked to their account.

Open the Clients list

Tap More > Clients.

  • Switch between Active and Archived tabs.
  • Search by name, or tap the filter icon to filter by city, state, property type, or tags.
  • Tap + to add a client, or tap any row to open it.

Each row shows the client's name, type, phone, email, and tags.

Add a client

  1. Tap + on the Clients list.
  2. Step 1 – Client Info: enter the required First Name, Last Name, Phone, and Email. Optionally add a company name, website, attachments and tags.
  3. Step 2 – Property & Review: input the initial property details for the client (type, address, state, city, zip, and timezone), as well as billing information; an optional property image may be uploaded. Finally, review the summary and tap Add client.

Tip: You can turn on Billing address same as property to copy the address automatically.

Existing client check: as you enter the email or phone in Step 1, the app checks whether a client already exists with those details. If it finds a match, it prompts you so you don't create a duplicate — and lets you Send invite to give that client portal access ("Invite sent successfully," or "Invite already sent" if one is still pending).

Client details

Open a client to see their record, organized in tabs:

  • Properties — all of the client's properties.
  • Appointments, Estimates, and Job & Invoices — everything linked to that client.

From here you can also start a new job, estimate, or invoice with the client pre-filled, using the More menu.

Manage properties

On the Properties tab:

  • Tap + New Property to add one.
  • Tap a property to view it; use Show map to see its location.

Each property card shows the address, type, and image.

Notes and attachments

Notes and attachments exist in two places — make sure you're in the right one:

  • Client-level: from the client's page, tap More → Client Details, then use the Notes or Attachments tab.
  • Property-level: from the client's page, tap a property card to open it, then use its own Notes or Attachments tab.

Message a client

Tap Chat (or Start a Conversation from the list ⋮ menu) to open the Inbox conversation with that client.

Archive and restore

Use the row's ⋮ menu:

  • Archive — hides the client from active views but keeps all their history.
  • Restore — brings an archived client back.

Note: Archiving never deletes a client's appointments, estimates, jobs, or invoices.

Services & Products

Services and products are the items you add to jobs, estimates, and invoices. A service is labor you charge by the hour; a product is a physical item you sell with a cost, markup, and (optionally) inventory tracking. This article covers finding, creating, editing, and archiving both.

Your catalog has two separate lists — Services and Products — each with its own Active and Archived tabs. Items you create here become available to add to jobs, estimates, and invoices. An item is either Active or Archived, and can be marked Taxable and shown on the client portal.

Note: When you add a product to a job, estimate, or invoice, any product that's out of stock appears disabled and can't be selected until it's back in stock.

Open Services and Products

Both live under the More tab (bottom navigation):

  • Tap More > Services to open the Services list.
  • Tap More > Products to open the Products list.

On each list you can search, tap the filter icon to narrow results, switch between Active and Archived, and tap + to create a new item. Tap any row to open its detail.

A service row shows: name, SKU, Rate (cost per hour), whether it's Taxable, In Use, shown on the Client Portal, plus tags and groups.

A product row shows: image, name, SKU, Cost, Markup %, Item Price, whether it's Taxable and In Use, plus tags and groups.

Create a service

  1. On the Services list, tap +.
  2. Step 1 – Enter Details: fill in the required fields:
    • SKU (required)
    • Name (required)
    • Cost Per Hour (required)
    • Optionally add a Description and Tags.
    • Taxable is on by default; Show on client portal is off by default.
    • Tap Next.
  3. Step 2 – Attachments (optional): upload files by category — Specifications, Manual, Installation, Safety, or Other (PNG, PDF, or JPG).
  4. Tap Submit.

You'll see "Service Item successfully created" and the item appears in your list.

Create a product

  1. On the Products list, tap +.
  2. Fill in the required fields:
    • SKU (required)
    • Name (required)
    • Cost (required)
  3. Enter a Markup % — the Item Price is calculated automatically as cost plus markup.
  4. Optionally add a product image (JPEG/PNG/WEBP, up to 4 MB), description, tags, and choose a Vendor, Manufacturer.
  5. Set inventory details if you track stock: Unit of Measure, Quantity On Hand, Re-Order Quantity, and Location. To turn tracking off, enable Disable Inventory Tracking.
  6. Taxable is on by default. Tap Submit.

You'll see "Product successfully created."

View or edit an item

Tap a row to open its detail screen.

  • A service detail has two tabs — Details (cost per hour, description, tags, groups, client-portal setting) and Attachments.
  • A product detail shows its Item Price / Cost / Markup, catalog details (vendor, manufacturer, unit, groups, location), SKU, product image, Taxable and Active status, and inventory (tracking status, quantity on hand, re-order level).

Tap Edit to change the item, then Update to save. You can also Archive the item directly from this screen.

Note: In Use is set automatically — it shows "Yes" when the item is already used on a job, estimate, or invoice.

Filter your catalog

Tap the filter icon on either list to filter by:

  • Tags
  • Groups
  • Taxable — show only tax-applicable items
  • Client Portal — show only items visible in the client portal (Services only)

Tap Filter to apply, or Reset to clear. The filter icon shows a badge with how many filters are active.

Archive, restore, or delete

Use a row's ⋮ menu (or the buttons on the detail screen):

  • Archive — moves an active item to the Archived tab (confirmation required).
  • Restore — brings an archived item back to Active.
  • Delete — permanently removes an archived item (confirmation required).

Note: You can only delete items that are archived first.

Quick-add a vendor and manufacturer

While creating or editing a product, you can add supporting records on the spot without leaving the form. Next to the Vendor and Manufacturer pickers, tap + Quick add:

  • Vendor — a supplier you buy from. Captures a Name.
  • Manufacturer — the maker of the product. Captures a Company Name.

The new record is saved and selected in the product form automatically.

Timesheets

Timesheets track the hours you and your crew work. This article covers reviewing your hours, creating timesheets and time entries, and managing their status.

A timesheet groups time entries for a user. Most entries are created automatically by the job and appointment timers (En Route, Job Time, Office Time, Job Prep), but you can also add and edit them by hand. Timesheets move through Draft, Pending, Approved, and Rejected.

Note: Once a timesheet is Approved, its entries are locked from editing. On the Solo plan, the Crew field is hidden throughout Timesheets (a single crew is used automatically).

Open Timesheets

Tap More > Timesheets. Use search and the filter icon to find timesheets, and tap + to create one.

Read the summary

The Summary card at the top shows your totals: Total hours, Total en route hours, Total office time, and Total job time.

Create a timesheet

  1. Tap + on the Timesheets screen.
  2. Pick a Date and, if you have a team, a User.
  3. Optionally add a Description.
  4. Tap Save.

Add a time entry

  1. Open a timesheet and tap Add time.
  2. Pick the Date, then the Job or User.
  3. Choose the entry type — En route, Job time, Office hours, or Job prep.
  4. Set the From and To times and add a Description.
  5. Tap Save.

Note: The end time must be after the start time and can't be in the future.

Timesheet details

Open a timesheet to see its Overview (date, crew, user) and its Time Entries. Each entry shows its job, type, and duration. Use an entry's ⋮ menu to view its description, edit, unlink, or delete it. Change the timesheet's status using the status chip at the top.

Filter timesheets

Tap the filter icon to filter by date range (with presets like This week, Last week, Last 30 days), user, and status.

Inbox (Messaging)

The Inbox is where you message clients and your team. This article covers starting conversations, switching between client and team chats, and sending messages.

The Inbox keeps your conversations in one place. Each conversation is tied to a client and can be a direct chat or attached to a specific job, estimate, or invoice.

Open the Inbox

Tap More > Inbox. You'll see your conversations with the latest message preview and time. Use the Active and Archived tabs and the search field to find a chat, and tap one to open it.

Start a new chat

  1. Tap + on the Inbox.
  2. Select a client (or use New client to add one).
  3. Type your message.
  4. Tap Send message.

You can also start a conversation from a client's record — tap Chat (or Start a Conversation from the client list).

Client vs. team conversations

Inside a conversation, two tabs let you choose who you're talking to:

  • Client — messages the client sees.
  • Team — internal messages for your team only.

Note: On the Solo plan, only the client conversation is shown.

Send a message

Type in the message bar and tap Send (the button reads Send to client or Send to team depending on the tab). You can attach files to a message.

Archived conversations

Archived chats appear under the Archived tab so your active list stays focused.

AI Assistant

The AI Assistant is a built-in helper you can talk to in plain language — by text, voice, or photo — to look things up and get answers about your business. This article covers how to use it on mobile.

The AI Assistant understands everyday language in English and Spanish and replies right in the chat. It's on the AI slot of the bottom navigation bar.

Open the AI Assistant

Tap the AI button (sparkle icon) in the bottom navigation. You'll see a welcome screen with a few example prompts to get started.

Ask by text

Type your question or request in the input bar — for example, "Show my jobs for today" — and tap Send. The assistant replies in the chat and may show suggested follow-up actions below its answer.

Ask by voice

Tap the microphone button and speak your request. The assistant transcribes what you said, answers, and reads its reply back to you.

Ask with a photo

Tap the camera / image button to attach up to 5 photos (JPG, JPEG, or WEBP), then send. The assistant can analyze the image — useful for reading a note, identifying a product, or documenting a job site. If you don't add text, it will describe or analyze the image by default.

Suggested prompts

On the welcome screen, tap any of the example tiles to send it instantly:

  • Show my jobs for today
  • Create new estimate
  • Show unpaid invoices
  • Schedule appointment

Tips

  • Be specific — include names, dates, or numbers when you can.
  • Use follow-ups — the assistant remembers the conversation, so you can say "which of those are unpaid?" without repeating yourself.
  • Switch languages freely — write in English or Spanish; it adapts automatically.
  • Double-check actions — confirm the assistant did what you intended, especially when it creates or changes a record.

Quick Add

Quick Add lets you create common records fast, with just the essential fields, from anywhere in the app. This article covers what you can quick-add and how it works.

Quick Add is the fastest way to create a record without opening a full form. Every quick-add captures only the must-have fields and marks the record as incomplete, so you can finish the details later.

Use Quick Add

  1. Tap the ⚡ Quick Add button in the bottom navigation.
  2. Choose what to create from the grid.
  3. Fill in the essential fields and tap Save.

You'll get a confirmation like "New Client added using Quick Add," often with a View shortcut to open the record.

Note: Records created via Quick Add show a small "finish setup" badge in their list until you complete the missing details.

What you can create

From the Quick Add grid you can create:

  • Appointment
  • Client
  • Job
  • Estimate
  • Crew (hidden on Solo plan)
  • User (hidden on Solo plan)
  • Service item (a service or a product)
  • Property
  • Payment

Inviting a client

When you quick-add a client, as you enter their email or phone the app checks whether they already exist. You can Send invite to give them portal access — you'll see "Invite sent successfully," or "Invite already sent" if one is still pending.

Quick-adds inside other forms

You'll also find + Quick add buttons inside pickers throughout the app, so you never have to leave what you're doing. For example, while adding a product you can quick-add a Vendor (name), Manufacturer (company name), or Group (name and code) — and while scheduling a job you can quick-add a client, property, or crew on the spot.

Notifications

The app keeps you informed with push notifications and an in-app alerts list. This article covers where to find them and how they work.

Notifications alert you to things that need attention — new job assignments, incoming messages, payment and invoice updates, timesheet approvals, and reminders. They arrive as push notifications on your device and also collect in an in-app Alerts list.

See your notifications

When you have unread alerts, a badge appears on the Home icon in the navigation bar. Open the dashboard and tap the bell icon to see the full list. Pull down to refresh, and scroll to load older ones.

Each notification shows an icon for its type (job, estimate, invoice, appointment, timesheet, and so on), the time it arrived, and its message. Unread notifications are highlighted with a colored dot.

Open a notification

Tap a notification to mark it read and jump straight to the related record — for example, a job, estimate, invoice, or appointment.

Mark as read

Tap the mark-all-as-read button (open-envelope icon) at the top of the Alerts screen to clear all unread notifications at once. Tapping any single notification also marks just that one as read.

Settings

Settings is where you manage your business configuration from the app — your invoice numbering and tax rates, your working availability, and which notifications you receive. This article covers the Settings hub and its three areas: Business, Availability, and Notifications.

Settings lives under the More tab, in the App & Support group. The Settings hub is a simple menu with three tiles — Business, Availability, and Notifications — each opening its own screen. Changes are saved per screen and take effect immediately once you tap Save.

Open Settings

  1. Tap More (bottom navigation).
  2. Under App & Support, tap Settings.
  3. Choose Business, Availability, or Notifications.

Business Settings

Tap Settings > Business to manage your invoice numbering and tax rates.

Invoice Number Counter — the number your next invoice will use.

  • Type a value directly, or use the up/down stepper to change it by one (it can't go below 0).
  • A Save / Cancel footer appears inside the counter card only when the value differs from what's saved. Cancel reverts it; Save stores the new counter and shows "Business Settings successfully updated."

Manage Tax Rates — tap to open a bottom sheet listing your account's tax rates, so you can review and manage them.

Note: If the screen fails to load, tap Retry. This is a focused, mobile version of Business Settings — it doesn't include your company name, address, or logo (manage those on the web).

Availability

Tap Settings > Availability to set the days and hours you're available, plus any exceptions.

Work Days — one card per day, Monday through Sunday:

  • Toggle a day on to work that day (a default 8:00 AM–5:00 PM slot is added) or off to remove all its slots.
  • Each slot has a From and To time — tap either to pick a time.
  • Tap + Add Time Slot to add another slot (up to 5 per day). Tap the X to remove a slot (you can't remove the last one).

Exceptions — periods when all your services are unavailable. Tap the + to add one. You can have up to three, one of each type:

  • By Day Range — a start and end date. Turn on Annual Exception to repeat it every year.
  • By Month — pick one or more months (Jan–Dec).
  • By Season — pick Winter, Spring, Summer, and/or Fall.

A live summary shows exactly what will be blocked. Tap Add Exception to save it, or the trash icon on a card to remove it.

Save your changes: the Reset and Save buttons at the bottom light up only when you have unsaved changes. Reset discards edits; Save shows "Availability successfully updated."

Notification Settings

Tap Settings > Notifications to control which alerts you get and where.

Each notification category has two switches — Email and Mobile (push) — that you toggle independently. The categories are:

  • Financial
  • Jobs
  • Estimates
  • Appointments
  • Timesheets
  • Inventory
  • Team
  • Messages
  • Feedback

Tap Save to apply ("Notifications successfully updated"), or Reset to undo pending changes. Both buttons enable only when you've made a change.

Note: These switches control Email and Mobile push only; any other channels configured for your account are left as they are.
