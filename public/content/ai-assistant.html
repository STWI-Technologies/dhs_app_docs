AI Assistant

Overview

The AI Assistant is your intelligent helper built directly into the Direct Home Service app. It understands natural language in both English and Spanish, so you can simply type or send a photo to get things done — no menus to navigate, no forms to fill out.

The AI Assistant is available on both the web app and the mobile app (iOS and Android). It uses the same powerful AI engine as the DHS WhatsApp integration, but is accessed through a dedicated chat interface within the app itself.

Where to Find It

  • Web App — Look for the AI Assistant icon in the main navigation or sidebar.
  • Mobile App — Tap the AI Assistant button on the bottom navigation bar or home screen.

Getting Started

  1. Open the AI Assistant — Tap or click the AI Assistant icon from anywhere in the app.
  2. Choose your input method — Type a message or tap the camera/upload icon to send a photo.
  3. Ask your question or give a command — Use natural, everyday language. For example: "Show me today's appointments" or "Create a new client named Maria Lopez."
  4. Review the response — The assistant will reply with the information you requested, confirm actions taken, or ask follow-up questions if needed.

There is no special syntax to learn. Just describe what you need the same way you would tell a coworker.

Opening Things in the App from Chat

The assistant understands four kinds of commands:

  • Open — returns a one-tap link to the app.
  • Show / View — displays results inline in the chat. No link. ("view" is an alias for "show".)
  • Create — creates a record directly in the chat; the assistant prompts for any missing details. No link.
  • Open Create — returns a one-tap link to the blank create form, for the times you would rather fill out the page yourself.

When the assistant sends you a link (Open or Open Create), that link does three things at once:

  1. Logs you in automatically — no password screen, no waiting.
  2. Drops you exactly where you need to be — the right job, list, or form is already loaded.
  3. Stays clean in your messages — it is a short link that fits in a single text bubble.

1. Open — Jump to a specific record

Use this when you want to open a specific job, estimate, invoice, client, or other record you have been discussing.

You Say What Happens
"open it" Opens whatever you were just talking about
"open this" Opens the most recent item from your chat
"open 3" After the assistant lists 5 jobs, opens the third one
"open the Henderson estimate" Looks up the Henderson estimate by name and opens it
"chat with Maria" Opens your inbox conversation with that client
"open chat with Maria" Same — both phrasings open the inbox with that client

Why both chat phrasings work: the AI assistant cannot carry out a live chat with a client directly — the actual conversation happens in the DHS inbox. So any "chat with [name]" request opens the inbox page for that client, whether or not you prefix it with "open".

Why use it? You are mid-conversation about a specific record and want to see it, edit it by hand, attach a photo, or just confirm what you are looking at. One tap and you are there.

2. Open — Browse an entire section

Use this when you want to browse a whole section of the app instead of any one specific record.

You Say What Happens

"open estimates" Opens the Estimates list page
"open my clients" Opens the Clients list page
"open invoices" Opens the Invoices list page
"the appointments page" Opens the Appointments list page

Why use it? Sometimes you do not have a specific record in mind — you just want to scroll, filter, search, or get an overview. List mode gets you to the section landing page in one tap.

Note: If you say "show me my invoices" or "list my jobs", the assistant will display the records inline in the chat — no link. Use "open" (as in "open invoices") when you want the full list page in the app.

3. Open Create — Open the blank form page

Use this when you would rather fill out the form by hand in the app instead of dictating the record to the AI. Say "open create" followed by the entity type. This is the only phrasing that returns a form link — bare "new estimate" or "create a job" triggers the AI to capture details in chat (see "Create records inline vs. Open Create", below).

"open create estimate" Opens the Estimates page with the new estimate form ready
"open create invoice" Opens the Invoices page with the new invoice form ready
"open create client" Opens the Clients page with the new client form ready
"open create property" Opens the Properties page with the new property form ready
"open create appointment" Opens the Appointments page with the new appointment form ready

Why use it? Some records are easier to create with the full form in front of you — especially when there are dropdowns, file attachments, multiple line items, or custom fields. Open Create skips the navigation and the "+ Add" click for you.

Create records inline vs. Open Create

The assistant supports both creation workflows — pick the one that fits the task:

  • Create inline — "create [thing]" (no link). The AI asks for anything it needs (client, date, line items) right there in the chat, then saves the record. Best when you already know the basics and want the record created instantly without leaving the conversation.
  • Open Create — "open create [thing]" (link). The AI sends you a one-tap link to the blank form page in the app. Best for complex records with attachments, photos, multiple line items, or custom fields — or when you want to look up details as you go.

What the Link Looks Like

Every link the assistant sends will appear in this format:

Henderson Roof Repair
https://dhs.app/s/k4n9p2

The line in italics tells you what the link will open. The URL underneath is what you tap. If you ever see the assistant write a URL in the middle of a sentence, that is a bug, not a real link — please report it.

Important Things to Know

  • Links are one-use only. The first tap logs you in and opens the app. The second tap will not work — you will see a friendly "Link Expired" page asking you to request a new one. If you tap twice by accident, just ask the assistant to open the same thing again for a brand-new link.
  • Links expire after 24 hours. Even if you never tap a link, it stops working after a day. Ask the assistant for a fresh one.
  • Links are tied to YOUR account. Forwarding a link to a coworker will not log them in — each team member needs to request their own links.
  • Links keep your URL bar clean. When you tap an Open Create link, the app opens the form and cleans the ?add=true parameter out of the URL automatically.
  • Works on every channel. The same link works whether you tap it from WhatsApp, SMS, the in-app chat panel, or a web browser preview. If you are already logged in, the link still works — it just skips the login step.

Tips and Tricks

  • 'Open it' is smarter than you think. The assistant remembers what you were just talking about. After "find jobs at the Henderson place", you can say "open it" (most recent), "open 2" (second in the list), or "open the roof one" (matches by keyword).
  • Numbered lists are your friend. When the assistant shows a numbered list of results, just type the number to act on it.
  • Use list mode when you want to browse. "open invoices" is faster than typing search keywords if you are not sure what you are looking for.
  • Use Open Create for complex records. "open create estimate" opens the full form page — use it when you need attachments, multiple line items, or custom fields.
  • Mix it with other AI features. Example workflow: "find clients in Tucson" → "open 4" → tap link, view notes → tap back → "create a job for them at 555 Oak Ave".

Image Analysis

You can upload or capture photos and the AI Assistant will analyze them. This is useful in many field service scenarios:

  • Job site documentation — Take a photo of completed work and ask the assistant to describe it or attach it to a job.
  • Document reading — Photograph a handwritten note, a printed form, or a business card and ask the assistant to extract the information.
  • Equipment identification — Snap a picture of equipment or a product label and ask the assistant to identify it.
  • Issue reporting — Send a photo of a problem at a job site and ask for guidance or to log it in the system.

To send an image, tap the camera or upload icon in the chat interface, then select or capture your photo.

Bilingual Support

The AI Assistant provides full support for both English and Spanish. You do not need to change any settings — just communicate in whichever language you prefer.

  • Automatic language detection — The assistant detects whether you are writing in English or Spanish and responds in the same language.

  • Mixed-language support — If you switch languages mid-conversation, the assistant adapts.

Tips & Example Conversations

The AI Assistant works best when you use natural, conversational language. Here are some tips and examples to help you get the most out of it.

General Tips

  • Be specific — Include names, dates, and job numbers when possible. "Show me John Smith's invoices" is better than "Show me invoices."
  • Use follow-ups — The assistant remembers your conversation context. After asking "Show me today's jobs," you can say "Which ones are incomplete?" without repeating yourself.
  • Ask for help — If you are unsure what the assistant can do, just ask: "What can you help me with?"
  • Combine requests — You can make complex requests like "Create a job for Maria Lopez at 123 Main St for next Monday at 9 AM and assign Crew A."

Security & Privacy

Your data security is a top priority. Here is how the AI Assistant keeps your information safe:

  • Authenticated access — The assistant can only access data if you are logged in with valid credentials.
  • No data sharing — Your queries and data are not shared with other accounts or used to train external models.
  • Role-based permissions — The assistant respects your user role. If you do not have permission to perform an action in the app, you cannot perform it through the assistant either.
  • Confirmation prompts — Destructive actions (like deleting records) always require explicit confirmation before they are executed.
