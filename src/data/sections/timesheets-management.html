Timesheets Management

Overview

Timesheets are how you track working hours for your team. Each timesheet belongs to a specific user and a specific date, and contains one or more time entries that record what the person did and for how long. Time entries are categorized by type — whether the person was traveling to a job site (en route), performing work at a job site (job time), or working in the office (office time).

Timesheets follow an approval workflow: they start as a Draft, get Submitted by the user for review, and are then either Approved or Rejected by an administrator or owner. This process ensures that hours are reviewed and verified before they are finalized.

From this section you can:

  • Create new timesheets for any user and date.
  • Add time entries to track specific blocks of work, travel, or office time.
  • Link and unlink time entries between timesheets.
  • Submit timesheets for approval.
  • Approve or reject timesheets (admins and owners only).
  • Delete timesheets and time entries (with restrictions on approved timesheets).
  • View analytics with summary cards showing total hours, en route time, office time, and job time.
  • Search and filter by date range, user, and status.

Important: Once a timesheet is approved, it becomes read-only. You cannot edit, delete, or unlink time entries from an approved timesheet. This protects finalized records from accidental modifications.

Controls and Filters

Below the summary cards you will find:

  • A New Timesheet button — click to open the create timesheet form.
  • A Search bar — type a keyword to search across timesheets (with a slight delay for performance).
  • A Date Range filter — select a predefined range or custom date range. Defaults to "All Time."
  • A User filter (hidden for crew members) — a multi-select dropdown to filter timesheets by specific users. Shows up to 100 users.
  • A Status filter — a popover with checkboxes for Draft, Pending, Approved, and Rejected. Shows a badge with the count of selected filters. Includes a "Reset filter" button to clear all selections.

Note: Crew members can only see their own timesheets. The User filter is hidden for crew members since they cannot view other people's timesheets. Admins and Owners can see all timesheets across the account.

Editing a Time Entry

Click the Edit (pencil icon) on any time entry to open the same Add Time modal pre-filled with the entry's current data. Modify any field and click Save to update it.

On success: "Time entry successfully updated."

Note: You cannot edit time entries on an approved timesheet. The edit button is disabled for approved entries.

Linking and Unlinking Time Entries

Time entries can exist independently (unlinked) or be associated with a specific timesheet (linked). This flexibility allows you to create time entries first and organize them into timesheets later.

Linking an Unlinked Entry

  1. Go to the timesheet detail page and switch to the Unlinked Entries tab.
  2. Click the Link icon on the entry you want to add.
  3. A popover appears with two options:
  • Link — Immediately links the entry to the current timesheet.
  • Select Another — Opens a modal where you can choose an existing timesheet or create a new one to link the entry to.

On success: "Time entry successfully linked."

Link Entry Modal (Select Another)

When you click Select Another, a modal opens with two options:

Option

What It Does

Existing Timesheet

Search for and select an existing timesheet from a dropdown. Shows timesheets as "User Name - Date" format.

Create New Timesheet

Create a new timesheet on the spot by selecting a date and user, then link the entry to it.

Unlinking a Linked Entry

  1. On the Linked Entries tab, click the Unlink icon on the entry.
  2. A confirmation dialog appears with the title Unlink Time Entry and a confirmation message.
  3. Click Unlink to confirm, or Cancel to go back.

On success: "Time entry successfully unlinked." The entry moves to the Unlinked Entries tab.

Warning: You cannot unlink entries from an approved timesheet. The unlink button is disabled for approved timesheets.

Timesheet Approval Workflow

Timesheets follow a status-based workflow that ensures hours are reviewed before they are finalized. The flow works as follows:

Status Flow

Draft → (Submit) → Pending → (Approve) → Approved

Pending → (Reject) → Rejected

Pending → (Withdraw) → Draft

Deleting Timesheets and Time Entries

Deleting a Timesheet

To delete a timesheet, click the Delete (trash icon) on the timesheet row in the list view. A confirmation dialog appears.

On success: "Timesheet successfully deleted." On failure: "Failed to delete timesheet."

Warning: You cannot delete a timesheet that has been Approved. The delete button is disabled and the tooltip reads: "This timesheet has been approved and cannot be deleted."

Deleting a Time Entry

You can delete time entries from the Unlinked Entries tab by clicking the Delete (trash icon). The confirmation dialog title depends on the context:

  • For unlinked entries: Title is "Permanently Delete Time Entry" with a message explaining the entry will be permanently removed.
  • For linked entries: Title is "Delete Time Entry" with a message explaining the entry will be removed from the timesheet.

On success: "Time entry successfully deleted." On failure: "Failed to delete time entry."

Cross-Platform Usage

Jobs

Time entries are tied to specific jobs through the Job field. When you select a job for a time entry (En Route or Work Time type), the entry becomes associated with that job's record. Job names appear in the time entry table's Job column.

Users and Crews

Each timesheet belongs to a specific user. The timesheet detail page shows the user's crew affiliation. When viewing crew-related timesheets, crew names appear with their assigned color indicators.

Reports

Timesheet data feeds into the Reports section, where you can view daily and monthly views of hours worked. Reports offer additional breakdowns by employee, date range, job, and client, and support PDF generation for printing or sharing.
