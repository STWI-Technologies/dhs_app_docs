Invoices Management

Overview

The Invoices Management section is where you create, send, and track invoices for your clients. An invoice is a billing document generated from a job, listing the services performed and products used along with pricing, taxes, discounts, and payment terms. The system supports the full invoice lifecycle from draft creation through client delivery, payment recording, and completion.

From this section you can:

  • Create new invoices linked to jobs, with service and product line items auto-populated from the job.
  • Edit invoice details, services, products, taxes, discounts, and deposit requirements.
  • Send invoices to clients via email and/or SMS with customizable messages.
  • Track status through the invoice lifecycle (Draft, Sent, Viewed, Paid).
  • Record payments with multiple payment methods and statuses.
  • Download PDF copies of invoices for offline sharing or records.
  • Delete invoices that are no longer needed.
  • Communicate with clients through built-in chat, notes, and attachments.
  • Add comments visible to clients on the invoice document.

Important: Once an invoice is marked as Paid, it becomes fully read-only. You cannot edit services, products, billing details, or invoice information. You can still add, edit, or delete payments and use the communication tools (chat, notes, attachments).

Invoices List View

The Invoices List View is the main view for managing all your invoices. To access it, navigate to Menu > Invoices.

When you open the Invoices List View, you will see invoices grouped by job. Each job group shows the job name as a header, a "Job Details" button to navigate to that job, and the total amount for all invoices in that group. Within each group, invoices are displayed in a table with the following columns:

Column

What It Means

Number

The auto-generated invoice number. Click the number to navigate to the Invoice Detail View. If the invoice was created via Quick Add (incomplete), a warning icon appears next to the number indicating additional information is needed.

Issue Date

The date the invoice was issued, formatted as Mon DD, YYYY (e.g., "Feb 10, 2026"). Shows "—" if no issue date was set.

Due Date

The date payment is due, formatted as Mon DD, YYYY. Shows "—" if no due date was set.

Total Balance

Two lines: the top line shows the invoice total, and the bottom line shows the remaining balance. If fully paid, the balance shows $0.00 in green. If unpaid, the balance due is shown in red.

Status

The current status of the invoice, shown as a color-coded tag. If the invoice has a chat conversation, a chat icon also appears. A blue icon with a red badge indicates unread messages.

Actions

Quick-action buttons: edit (pencil icon to open detail view) and delete (trash icon).

Invoice Statuses

Every invoice has one of the following statuses. These statuses help you track where each invoice is in the billing process:

Status

Meaning

The invoice has been sent to the client via email and/or SMS. The invoice can still be edited.

Sorting the List

You can sort the invoices list by clicking on the column headers for Number, Due Date, Total Balance, or Status. Click once to sort in ascending order and click again to sort in descending order.

Searching

Use the search bar at the top of the Invoices List View to search for invoices by keyword. Simply type your search term and the results will update automatically. The search resets to page 1 when the search term changes.

Filtering by Status

Click the "Status" filter button to open a popover with status checkboxes. You can select one or more statuses to show only invoices matching those statuses:

  • Draft
  • Sent
  • Viewed
  • Paid

When filters are active, a badge with the number of active filters appears on the button. Click "Reset filter" in the popover to clear all filters.

Pagination

The Invoices List View supports server-side pagination. You can change the number of invoices displayed per page (default is 10) and navigate between pages using the pagination controls at the bottom of the table.

Chat Icon in Status Column

If an invoice has an associated chat conversation, a chat icon appears next to the status tag:

  • Gray background — A chat exists but there are no unread messages. Clicking navigates to the Inbox.
  • Blue background with red badge — There are unread messages. The badge shows the count (up to "99+"). Clicking navigates to the Inbox with the conversation pre-selected.

1. Creating a New Invoice

To create a new invoice, follow these steps:

Step 1: Open the New Invoice Form

Click the "+ New Invoice" button (blue) located in the top-right corner of the Invoices List View. A drawer panel will slide open from the right side of the screen with a two-step wizard.

Tip: If the list is empty (no invoices have been created yet), you will see a "Create first invoice" button in the center of the page.

Step 2: Fill In the Invoice Details (Step 1 of 2)

The first step of the wizard is titled "Invoice Details" and contains the following fields:

Field

Required?

Description

Details

No

A text area (3 rows) for entering a description or notes regarding the invoice. Placeholder: "Enter description or details regarding this invoice".

Client

Yes

Select the client this invoice is for from the dropdown. You can search by company name or email. If the client does not exist yet, use the Quick Add option to create one without leaving the form.

Property

Yes

Select the property where the work was performed. Filtered by the selected client. Disabled until a client is selected (shows "Select client first"). You can Quick Add a new property.

Job

Yes

Select the job this invoice is associated with. Filtered by the selected client and property. Disabled until a property is selected (shows "Select property first"). You can Quick Add a new job. When a job is selected, its existing services and products are automatically loaded into Step 2.

Issue Date

Yes

The date the invoice is issued. Defaults to today's date. Cannot be after the due date.

Due Date

Yes

The date payment is due. Must be on or after the issue date.

After filling in all required fields, click "Next Step" to proceed. The system validates your entries — if any required field is missing, an error message appears next to that field.

Note: The cascading dropdown pattern means you must select a Client first, then a Property, then a Job. Each dropdown filters based on the previous selection.

Step 3: Review Service Items (Step 2 of 2)

The second step is titled "Service Items" and shows services and products that were automatically loaded from the selected job. You can:

  • Review the auto-populated services and products from the job.
  • Add additional services or products from your catalog.
  • Edit quantities, hours, rates, and prices for any item.
  • Remove items you don't want to include on this invoice.

Step 4: Save the Invoice

You have two options for saving:

  • "Create Invoice" (blue button) — Saves the invoice with all service items and products. A loading indicator appears while the operation completes.
  • "Skip" (text button) — Creates the invoice without any service items. You can add items later from the Invoice Detail View.

Both options create the invoice in Draft status. On success, a toast message appears: "Invoice created successfully". The new invoice will appear in your Invoices List View.

Click "Back" to return to Step 1 to modify the invoice details before saving.

2. Invoice Detail View

The Invoice Detail View is where you manage all aspects of a single invoice. To access it, click on any invoice's number or edit icon in the Invoices List View.

The Detail View has two main areas:

  • Main content area (left) — Contains the Job Details and Payments tabs.
  • Client sidebar (right) — Shows client information and communication tools (Chat, Notes, Attachments).

Header Area

The header displays:

  • Back button — Returns to the Invoices List View. If you arrived from an Appointment or Estimate, it returns you there instead.
  • Invoice number/name — The title of the invoice.
  • Status dropdown — A color-coded tag showing the current status. Click to change the status (see Section 5).
  • Send Invoice button — Opens the Send Invoice modal (see Section 6). Disabled if the invoice is Paid or has no service items.
  • More actions menu (three-dot icon) — Contains: Edit Invoice, Download PDF, and Delete. Edit and Delete are hidden when the invoice is Paid.

2.1 Job Details Tab

The Job Details tab is the default tab and displays all the invoice's editable content.

For Paid Invoices

When an invoice is in Paid status, the Job Details tab shows a read-only PDF-style preview of the invoice. No editing is allowed. All information is displayed in a formatted layout.

For Unpaid Invoices (Editable)

When the invoice is in Draft, Sent, Viewed, or Payment in Progress status, the following sections are displayed:

A text area where you can write a message that will appear on the invoice document when the client views it. Features:

  • A "Save" button appears when you modify the text (only enabled when text is not empty)
  • Disabled when the invoice is Paid
  • Help text: "This text will be displayed in the invoice"

4. Deleting an Invoice

Invoices can be permanently deleted from two places:

From the Invoices List View

  1. Locate the invoice in the list.
  2. Click the delete icon (trash icon) in the Actions column.
  3. A confirmation dialog appears with the title "Delete Invoice" and the message: "Are you sure you want to delete this invoice?"
  4. Click "Delete" (red button) to confirm, or "Cancel" to go back.

From the Invoice Detail View

  1. Open the Invoice Detail View.
  2. Click the three-dot menu in the header.
  3. Select "Delete".
  4. Confirm in the dialog.
  5. After deletion, you are redirected to the Invoices List View.

Note: Invoices in Paid status cannot be deleted. The delete option is hidden from the menu when the invoice is Paid.

Warning: Permanent deletion cannot be undone. Once an invoice is deleted, all of its information, service items, products, payments, notes, and attachments are removed from the system permanently.

5. Changing Invoice Status

You can change an invoice's status using the status dropdown in the Invoice Detail View header. Click the color-coded status tag to open the dropdown, then select the new status.

Available Statuses

  • Draft (purple) — Initial state, fully editable.
  • Sent (orange) — Sent to client, still editable.
  • Viewed (gray) — Client has viewed, still editable.
  • Paid (green) — Fully paid, becomes read-only.

While the status is being updated, a spinner appears with the message "Updating status to [new status]...". On success, a toast appears: "Invoice successfully updated".

Paid Status Restrictions

When an invoice is marked as Paid:

  • The Job Details tab shows a read-only PDF-style preview instead of editable fields.
  • The "Edit Invoice" and "Delete" options are hidden from the three-dot menu.
  • The "Send Invoice" button is hidden.
  • Services, products, and billing details cannot be modified.
  • The Comments to Client field is disabled.
  • Payments can still be added, edited, or deleted.
  • Chat, Notes, and Attachments remain fully functional.

6. Sending an Invoice to a Client

When your invoice is ready for the client, you can send it via email and/or SMS.

Step 1: Open the Send Invoice Modal

From the Invoice Detail View, click the "Send Invoice" button in the header. A full-screen drawer opens with the submission form.

Note: The Send Invoice button is disabled if the invoice has no service items. Tooltip: "At least one service item required to send invoice".

Step 2: Configure Delivery Channels

7. Recording Payments

Payments are recorded in the Payments tab of the Invoice Detail View. You can record payments even when the invoice is in Paid status.

7.1 Adding a Payment

  1. Navigate to the Payments tab.
  2. Click the "Add Payment" button.
  3. A drawer opens with the following fields:

Field

Required?

Description

Amount

Yes

The payment amount in dollars. Enter as a decimal (e.g., 250.00). Must be greater than 0.

Transaction Date

Yes

The date the payment was received. Defaults to today.

Payment Method

Yes

Select from: Check, Cash, Credit Card, Bank Transfer, or Other.

Status

Yes

The payment status. Options: Completed (default), Pending, Failed, Refunded, Voided.

Payment Reference

No

A reference number such as a check number, transaction ID, or confirmation code.

Notes

  1. Click "Add Payment" to save. On success: "Payment added to invoice".

7.2 Editing a Payment

  1. In the Payments History table, click the edit icon (pencil) for the payment.
  2. The same drawer opens, pre-filled with the payment's current data. The title changes to "Edit Payment".
  3. Make your changes and click "Update Payment". On success: "Payment successfully updated".

7.3 Deleting a Payment

  1. In the Payments History table, click the delete icon (trash) for the payment.
  2. A confirmation dialog appears: "Are you sure you want to delete this payment?"
  3. Click "Delete" to confirm. On success: "Payment successfully deleted".

8. Downloading an Invoice as PDF

To download a PDF copy of an invoice:

  1. Open the Invoice Detail View.
  2. Click the three-dot menu in the header.
  3. Select "Download PDF".
  4. The system generates and downloads a PDF file named invoice_[InvoiceID].pdf.

The PDF can be shared with clients via other channels (print, email attachments, etc.) or kept for your records.

9. Tax Management

Tax rates are managed from within the Bill section of an invoice. Click the settings icon next to the tax rate dropdown to open the Tax Management Modal.

From this modal you can:

  • View all saved tax rates.
  • Create a new tax rate by entering a name and percentage.
  • Edit existing tax rates.
  • Delete tax rates that are no longer needed.
  • Select a tax rate to apply it to the current invoice.

Tax rates are stored as basis points (e.g., 850 = 8.50%) and displayed as percentages. The tax is calculated only on taxable items — the backend determines the taxable total and applies the rate.

10. Searching and Filtering Invoices

10.1 Quick Search

The search bar at the top of the Invoices List View allows you to search by keyword. Type your search term and the results update automatically. The search resets to page 1.

10.2 Status Filter

Click the "Status" button to open the filter popover. Select one or more statuses:

Filter Option

What It Shows

Draft

Invoices being prepared, not yet sent.

Sent

Invoices sent to clients, awaiting payment.

Viewed

Invoices the client has opened and viewed.

Paid

Invoices that have been fully paid.

A badge shows the number of active filters. Click "Reset filter" to clear all.

Tip: Combine search and status filters for precise results. For example, filter by "Sent" to see all outstanding invoices awaiting payment.
